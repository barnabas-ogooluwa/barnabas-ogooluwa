Advanced Induction Welding:
Seamed Pipes & Square Tubes

Authored by Barnabas

Explore Research

Assignment Overview

This research project investigates the complexities of High-Frequency Induction (HFI) welding, a transformative joining technology that serves as the backbone of modern infrastructure. By leveraging electromagnetic principles, HFI welding enables the high-speed production of longitudinal seams with exceptional metallurgical integrity, critical for the global energy, construction, and automotive sectors.

Key Research Pillars

Process Physics

Analysis of Skin and Proximity effects, detailing how 150-450 kHz currents localize heat at the welding "V" for maximum precision and efficiency.

System Components

A deep dive into HFI infrastructure, from the high-output HF power supplies to the critical role of Ferrite Impedors in current focusing.

Manufacturing Flow

A comprehensive walkthrough of the production cycle: from the initial forming of steel strips to the final shaping of square and rectangular profiles.
